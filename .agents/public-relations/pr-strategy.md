# PR Strategy

Use when the user asks how to get press, where to pitch, how to launch, or how to turn company work into earned media.

## Strategy spine

1. Who must believe what for the next business milestone?
2. What is genuinely new, timely, or useful to that audience?
3. Why does this company have standing?
4. Which channel earns that belief: press, owned media, community, analyst, partner, creator, or direct founder narrative?
5. What proof can survive journalist scrutiny?

## Anti-patterns

- Naming outlets before audience.
- Treating a funding round, feature launch, or existence as automatically newsworthy.
- Measuring PR by impressions/AVE instead of right-audience belief and downstream action.
- Hiring or automating before the narrative is clear.

## Output

Return a concise plan:

- Audience and belief goal.
- Narrative/wedge.
- News peg or drumbeat calendar.
- Proof assets needed.
- Best channel mix.
- First PR action: newsworthiness check, direct founder post, media list, reactive monitoring, or hold.
