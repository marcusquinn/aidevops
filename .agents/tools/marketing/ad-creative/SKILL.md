## Core Principles

### Creative Performance Hierarchy

1. **Hook (First 3s of video / First line of copy)** — 40% of performance
2. **Visual Thumb-Stop** — 25%
3. **Offer/Value Proposition** — 20%
4. **Social Proof** — 10%
5. **Call to Action** — 5%

### 8 Principles of High-Converting Creative

1. **Pattern Interrupt** — Break the user's feed pattern
2. **Clarity Over Cleverness** — Clear messaging beats clever wordplay
3. **Benefit-First** — Lead with what the customer gets
4. **Specificity Sells** — "Increase conversions by 23%" > "Increase conversions significantly"
5. **Emotional First, Logical Second** — Buy on emotion, justify with logic
6. **Native to Platform** — Look like content, not an ad
7. **Mobile-First** — 80%+ of social ad views are mobile
8. **Test Everything** — Data over opinions

### Creative Funnel Framework

| Stage | Audience State | Creative Approach |
|-------|---------------|-------------------|
| Unaware (Cold) | Don't know the problem | Problem-focused hooks, education, curiosity gaps |
| Problem Aware | Feel the pain | Solution-focused, benefit-driven, "better way" |
| Solution Aware | Researching options | Feature differentiation, unique mechanism, proof |
| Product Aware | Evaluating you | Direct response, urgency, testimonials, risk reversal |
| Most Aware (Hot) | Ready to buy/rebuy | Reminders, cart abandonment, loyalty, upsell |

## Meta/Facebook Ads Creative

### Meta Ad Specs Quick Reference

| Placement | Format | Size | Ratio | Max File | Max Length |
|-----------|--------|------|-------|----------|------------|
| Feed | Image | 1080x1080px | 1:1 | 30 MB | N/A |
| Feed | Video | 1080x1080px | 1:1 or 4:5 | 4 GB | 240 min |
| Stories | Image | 1080x1920px | 9:16 | 30 MB | N/A |
| Stories | Video | 1080x1920px | 9:16 | 4 GB | 120 sec |
| Reels | Video | 1080x1920px | 9:16 | 4 GB | 60 sec |
| Carousel | Image | 1080x1080px | 1:1 | 30 MB | N/A |
| Carousel | Video | 1080x1080px | 1:1 | 4 GB | 240 min |
| Collection | Cover | 1200x628px | 1.91:1 | 30 MB | N/A |

**Copy limits:** Primary text — 125 chars before truncation on mobile. Headline — 40 chars. Description — 30 chars.

### Image Ads Best Practices

- High-quality visuals; avoid generic stock photos
- Create contrast against platform background; ensure text readable at small sizes
- One clear value proposition per image; mobile-first design
- Test compositions: close-up vs. wide, product-only vs. lifestyle, people vs. no people
- Text overlays: 5-7 words max, contrasting colors, avoid covering key visuals
- Color psychology: Red=urgency, Blue=trust, Yellow=optimism, Green=growth, Orange=confidence, Purple=luxury

**Image Ad Copy Formula:**

```
PRIMARY TEXT:
[Hook - First line that stops the scroll]
[Amplify - Expand on the hook, add context]
[Proof - Social proof, stats, testimonials]
[Offer - What they get, how they benefit]
[CTA - Clear next step]

HEADLINE: [Benefit-driven, complements primary text]
DESCRIPTION: [Supporting detail or urgency element]
```

**Example — E-commerce:**

```
PRIMARY TEXT:
Still using razors from the grocery store? 🪒

There's a reason 47,000+ men switched to our precision-engineered blades.
They're sharper, last longer, and cost 40% less.

• Smoother shave with less irritation
• Blades last 2-3x longer
• Save $15+ every month

First month: 50% off + free shipping

HEADLINE: Premium Razors at Store-Brand Prices
DESCRIPTION: Free returns. Cancel anytime.
CTA: Shop Now
```

### Video Ads Best Practices

1. **Hook in first 3 seconds** — pattern interrupts, questions, bold statements
2. **Design for sound-off** — 85% watch without sound; captions required
3. **Mobile-first framing** — Vertical (9:16) or square (1:1) > horizontal
4. **Fast pacing** — Cut every 2-4 seconds for feed content
5. **Show, don't tell** — Demonstrate product in use
6. **Native platform feel** — Feed=polished, Stories=raw, Reels=entertaining
7. **Clear CTA** — Visual + verbal + written + button

**Video length by objective:** Awareness 6-15s | Consideration 15-30s | Conversion 30-90s | Retargeting 6-20s

**Video Structure Formulas:**

```
15-SECOND:
0-3s: HOOK (pattern interrupt)
3-7s: PROBLEM/DESIRE
7-12s: SOLUTION (product in action)
12-15s: CTA (with urgency)

30-SECOND:
0-3s: HOOK
3-8s: RELATE (understand their problem)
8-15s: REVEAL (solution with proof)
15-25s: RESULTS (transformation)
25-30s: CTA (strong offer)

60-SECOND:
0-3s: HOOK (surprising fact/result)
3-10s: CONTEXT (why this matters)
10-25s: EDUCATION (how it works)
25-45s: PROOF (case study, testimonials)
45-55s: OFFER
55-60s: CTA
```

### Carousel Ads

- 2-10 cards, each with own link. First card most important.
- Consistent visual theme across cards; create visual flow
- **Story sequence:** Card 1=Hook/Problem → Cards 2-4=Solution/Features → Cards 5-6=Social Proof → Final=CTA/Offer
- Each headline should work independently and create curiosity to swipe

### Reel Ads

- 9:16 only, 9-15 seconds for max completion rate
- **Entertainment-first** — fun, interesting, inspiring; avoid hard-selling
- Use trending audio; sync visuals to beats
- Authentic creator style > polished brand content
- Sound is critical (unlike feed, Reels play with sound on)

```
9-SECOND REEL:
0-1s: HOOK (visual surprise or text)
1-4s: ENTERTAINMENT
4-7s: PRODUCT (subtle integration)
7-9s: SOFT CTA (in caption)
```

### Meta Ad Copy Best Practices

**Hook formulas:** Question ("Still [doing old way]?") | Bold claim | Negative ("Stop [mistake]") | Curiosity | Contrast | Story | Direct benefit

**Body structure:** Hook → Amplify → Proof → Offer → CTA

**Length guidelines:** Awareness 50-100 chars | Consideration 100-250 | Conversion 150-500

### Meta Creative Testing Framework

**What to test:** Ad format | Visual elements | Copy elements | Audience-message match | Offers

**Methodology:**

1. Test 3-5 creatives per ad set; run 3-7 days minimum
2. Minimum 50 conversions for significance
3. Isolate ONE variable at a time
4. Winner criteria: CPA/ROAS primary, CTR secondary, volume tertiary
5. Iterate on winners: create variations, test against control

## Google Ads Creative

Google is about answering intent (matching the search), not interruption (stopping the scroll).

### Responsive Search Ads (RSA)

**Specs:** 3-15 headlines (30 chars each), 2-4 descriptions (90 chars each), 2 path fields (15 chars each).

**Best practices:**

- Provide all 15 headlines and 4 descriptions
- Include top keyword in 2+ headlines; use dynamic keyword insertion: `{KeyWord:Default Text}`
- Each headline must stand alone and be unique
- Pin only when necessary (reduces optimization flexibility)
- Mix: keyword-rich, benefit-focused, offer/promo, social proof, CTAs

**RSA Headline Formula (15 Headlines):**

```
H1-3: Keyword-Rich — [Primary Keyword] - [Differentiator]
H4-6: Benefit-Focused — [Key Benefit] in [Timeframe]
H7-9: Offer/Promo — [Discount]% Off [Product] Today
H10-12: Social Proof — Trusted by [Number]+ [Customers]
H13-15: CTAs — Shop [Category] Now
```

**Description Formula (4 Descriptions):**

```
D1: Value prop + top benefits
D2: Social proof + CTA
D3: Offer + urgency + CTA
D4: Features + ease of use + support
```

**Advanced tactics:** IF functions `{IF(device=mobile):Text}` | Location insertion `{LOCATION(City)}` | Countdown `{COUNTDOWN(date)}`

**Optimization cycle:** Days 1-14 let Google test → Day 14+ review asset report → Replace "Low" performers → Maintain 10+ headlines, 3+ descriptions

### Responsive Display Ads (RDA)

**Specs:** Images: 1200x628 (landscape, required), 1200x1200 (square, required), up to 15 images. Logos: 1200x1200 (square, required). Videos: YouTube only, up to 5. Headlines: 5 short (30 char) + 1 long (90 char). Descriptions: up to 5 (90 char).

**Best practices:** Provide all slots. Mix product shots with lifestyle. Ensure images work at all sizes. Keep text minimal (<20% of image).

### Performance Max Asset Groups

**Specs:** Up to 20 images (landscape 1.91:1, square 1:1, portrait 4:5), 5 videos, 5 short headlines (30 char), 5 long headlines (90 char), 5 descriptions (90 char).

**Key rules:**

- Upload maximum assets — more = better performance across Search, Display, YouTube, Gmail, Discover, Maps
- Video is essential (dramatically improves performance)
- Create separate asset groups for different product categories/segments
- All three image aspect ratios required

### YouTube Ads

**In-Stream (Skippable):** Skip after 5s. First 5 seconds are everything — deliver value before skip. Front-load brand/product.

```
0-5s: Hook + brand/product + core benefit + reason to keep watching
5-15s: Expand benefit, show product
15-30s: Demonstrate value, provide proof
30-45s: Testimonial/results, clear offer, strong CTA
```

**Bumper (Non-Skippable):** Exactly 6 seconds. Single-minded message only.

```
0-2s: Hook/Visual | 2-4s: Product/Benefit | 4-6s: Brand/CTA
```

**Shorts:** 9:16 vertical, up to 60s, sound on by default. Entertainment-first, creator-style, trending audio.

## Copywriting Frameworks

### 1. PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

```
P: Identify the PROBLEM
A: AGITATE the pain (consequences, not just features)
S: Present the SOLUTION
```

**Example — B2B:**

```
P: "Wasting 10+ hours per week on manual data entry?"
A: "While you're buried in spreadsheets, your competitors are growing. Every hour of manual work is an hour not spent on strategy."
S: "AutoFlow eliminates 95% of manual data entry. 2,000+ companies saved 1,500 hours last month."
```

Best for: Cold audiences, clear pain points, competitive markets.

### 2. AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

```
A: Grab ATTENTION (relevant hook)
I: Build INTEREST (relevant information)
D: Create DESIRE (emotional picture)
A: Prompt ACTION (urgency + CTA)
```

Best for: E-commerce, lead gen, video scripts, sequential campaigns.

### 3. BAB (Before-After-Bridge)

```
B: BEFORE state (current pain — make it relatable)
A: AFTER state (desired outcome — aspirational but believable)
B: BRIDGE (your product/service — specific, not vague)
```

Best for: Transformation products, coaching, services.

### 4. The 4 Us Audit

Every piece of copy should be: **Useful** (provides value) + **Urgent** (reason to act now) + **Unique** (differentiated) + **Ultra-Specific** (concrete details).

**Weak:** "Get better results with our software"
**Strong:** "Reduce customer churn by 34% in 60 days with our predictive retention system — 15 spots left in March onboarding"

### 5. Star-Story-Solution

Introduce relatable character → Their journey/struggle → How they overcame it with your product. Best for: Video testimonials, case studies, email sequences.

### 6. Features → Benefits Translation

```
Feature: What it is → Advantage: What it does → Benefit: What it means for them

"Cooling gel foam" → "Regulates temperature" → "Wake up refreshed, not sweaty"
"AI email automation" → "Personalized emails on behavior" → "More sales while you sleep"
```

## UGC-Style Ad Creation

UGC-style = content that looks/feels like it was created by a real customer, shot on smartphone, casual setting, authentic delivery.

**Why UGC works:** Native to platform, higher trust, lower production cost, better engagement, algorithm-favored.

### UGC Creation Process

**Sourcing creators:** Hire (Fiverr, Billo, Insense — $100-500/video) | Customer testimonials (incentivize) | Internal team (fastest, free)

**UGC Brief Template:**

```
CREATOR: [Name/Type]
PRODUCT: [What they're promoting]
TARGET AUDIENCE: [Demographics, pain points, desires]
KEY MESSAGES: 1. [Main benefit] 2. [Differentiator] 3. [Social proof]

SCRIPT STRUCTURE:
0-3s: [Hook] | 3-10s: [Story/problem] | 10-20s: [Solution/product]
20-30s: [Benefits/results] | 30-40s: [Recommendation] | 40-45s: [CTA]

FILMING: Vertical 9:16, smartphone, natural lighting, casual setting
DELIVERY: Conversational, not scripted-sounding
DON'T: Sound like an ad, use marketing jargon, be overly promotional
DELIVERABLES: Raw footage + usage rights for paid advertising
```

**Filming best practices:** Smartphone vertical, natural light (face window), person fills 60-70% of frame, eye level, simple background, 3-5 full takes with varied hooks.

**Editing:** Minimal (maintain raw feel), jump cuts for energy, captions REQUIRED (large font, high contrast, synced), simple transitions. Tools: CapCut, InShot, Canva, VEED.io.

### UGC Script Frameworks

**1. Problem-Discovery-Solution:**

```
[0-3s] "I used to struggle with [pain point]"
[3-10s] "I tried [solution 1], [solution 2]... nothing worked"
[10-20s] "Then [timeframe] ago, [how you found product]"
[20-35s] "Within [timeframe], I noticed [specific result]"
[35-45s] "If you're dealing with [problem], honestly try this"
[CTA] "Link in bio" or "Use code [CODE]"
```

**2. Transformation Story:** Where you started → The struggle → Turning point → The journey (early result → bigger result → now) → Why it worked

**3. Comparison/Alternative:** "I switched from [popular] to [product]" → Why → What's better → Results → Recommendation

**4. Unexpected Benefit:** "I bought [product] for [reason] but [unexpected benefit]" → Original intent → Surprise discovery → Why it matters

**5. Direct Recommendation:** "If you [qualifier], you need this" → Why it's perfect for them → Features+benefits → Proof → How to get it

### UGC Scaling

Phase 1 (Weeks 1-2): 5 concepts, small budget, find winning angle
Phase 2 (Weeks 3-4): 10 variations of winner, different creators
Phase 3 (Weeks 5+): 5-10 creators/month, 20+ ads in rotation
Phase 4: Build creator roster, template briefs, streamline approval

## Creative Testing & Optimization

*Merged: Creative Testing Methodology, A/B Testing, Ad Creative Scoring*

### Testing Philosophy

1. Test ONE variable at a time
2. Volume matters — launch 5-10 creatives per test
3. Speed wins — weekly test launches, kill losers fast, scale winners immediately
4. Data over opinions

### Testing Levels

**Level 1 — Concept:** Different hooks/angles. Same format, audience, budget. 3-7 days minimum.
**Level 2 — Format:** Image vs. video vs. UGC vs. carousel. Same messaging.
**Level 3 — Element:** Headlines, primary text, CTAs, offers, thumbnails. ONE change at a time, 5+ variations.
**Level 4 — Audience-Message Match:** Same creative across segments, or segment-specific messaging.

### Variable Isolation

```
✅ GOOD: New video + Same headline + Same audience + Same offer
   → If it wins, you know it's the video

❌ BAD: New video + New headline + New audience + New offer
   → If it wins, you don't know why
```

### Statistical Significance

**Minimum thresholds:** Image/text: 50+ conversions/variant | Video: 30+ | Audience: 100+ | Format: 20+

**Confidence:** 95%+ = safe to declare winner | 75% = keep testing | 50% = meaningless

### Winner Identification

```
A creative is a "winner" if:
✅ CPA is 20%+ better than target
✅ Volume sufficient (>10 conversions/day potential)
✅ Statistical confidence >90%
✅ Performance stable (not declining)
```

**Scaling winners:**

```
Day 1-7: Test budget ($50-100/day)
Day 8-14: If winner, 2x budget
Day 15-21: If still performing, 2x again
Day 22+: 20-40% increases until performance degrades
```

**Iteration:** Don't stop at one winner. Create variations: different creator same script, same creator different hook, shorter version, different product focus, different editing style.

### Testing Frameworks

**Sequential:** Week 1 test headlines → Week 2 test images with winning headline → Week 3 test CTAs → Week 4 test offers

**Champion vs. Challengers:** 1 champion (40% budget) + 4-5 challengers (15% each). Weekly: best challenger beats champion? New champion. Worst challenger replaced.

**Bracket:** 8 variations → Top 4 get more budget → Top 2 battle → Winner gets full budget.

### Testing Calendar

**Weekly:** Mon=launch 5 new tests, Wed=check data/kill non-starters, Fri=analyze performance
**Monthly:** Creative audit, performance ranking, fatigue analysis, new angle brainstorming

### Pre-Launch Creative Scorecard (100 points)

| Category | Points | Key Criteria |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Hook Quality | 25 | Pattern interrupt, relevance, curiosity, clarity, specificity |
| Value Proposition | 20 | Benefit clarity, differentiation, proof, relevance |
| Creative Execution | 20 | Production quality, native feel, mobile optimization, branding |
| Copy Quality | 20 | Headline, primary text, CTA, tone/voice |
| Offer & CTA | 15 | Offer strength, urgency/scarcity, friction reduction |

**Score interpretation:** 90-100=Excellent | 75-89=Good, launch with tweaks | 60-74=Needs improvement | <60=Major revision

### Post-Launch Scorecard (after 7 days)

| Metric | Weight | Scoring |
|--------|--------|---------|
| CPA vs. Target | /30 | 30%+ better=30, 10-29%=25, at target=20, worse=0-15 |
| Volume | /20 | >20 conv/day=20, 10-19=15, 5-9=10, <5=5 |
| CTR | /15 | >3%=15, 2-3%=12, 1-2%=8, <1%=4 |
| Hook Rate | /15 | >60%=15, 50-60%=12, 40-49%=8, <40%=4 |
| Engagement | /10 | Above avg=10, at avg=7, below=4 |
| Longevity | /10 | Improving=10, stable=8, declining=5 |

**Action:** 85-100=Scale aggressively | 70-84=Scale moderately | 50-69=Keep testing | <50=Optimize or kill

### Creative Fatigue Detection

| Metric | Fresh | Fatigued |
|--------|-------|----------|
| CTR decline | Stable/rising | >20% from peak |
| CPA increase | Stable/decreasing | >25% from baseline |
| Frequency | <3 | >5 |
| Hook Rate | >50% | <35% |

**Refresh levels:**

- **Minor** (extend +7-14 days): Change headline, swap thumbnail, update offer
- **Moderate** (extend +14-30 days): New hook, different image/video, rewrite primary text
- **Major** (new 30-90 day lifespan): Entirely new concept, angle, format, creators

## Offer Construction

### Irresistible Offer Formula

```
CORE OFFER + BONUSES (increase perceived value) + URGENCY (time/quantity limit)
+ SCARCITY (limited availability) + RISK REVERSAL (guarantee) + SOCIAL PROOF
= IRRESISTIBLE OFFER
```

### Core Offer Types

| Type | When to Use | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **% Off** | Higher-priced products ($100+) | "50% Off Your First Month" |
| **$ Off** | Lower-priced products (<$100) | "$20 Off Your First Order" |
| **Free Trial** | Subscriptions, SaaS, services | "Try free 30 days, no credit card" |
| **Free Bonus** | E-commerce, info products | "Get bundle + free jade roller ($25 value)" |
| **BOGO** | Inventory clearance, consumables | "Buy 2, Get 1 Free" |
| **Bundle** | Complementary products, increase AOV | "Complete Bundle: $118 value for $79" |
| **Limited-Time** | Create urgency | "50% off ends in 8 hours" |
| **First-Time** | Customer acquisition | "New customers: 40% off + free shipping" |
| **Guarantee** | Higher-priced, unknown brands | "60-day money-back, no questions asked" |

### Bonus Stacking

```
CORE: $X value
+ BONUS 1: Related product ($Y value)
+ BONUS 2: Complementary item ($Z value)
+ BONUS 3: Exclusive access ($A value)
+ FAST ACTION BONUS: Order in [timeframe] ($B value)
TOTAL VALUE: $X+Y+Z+A+B | YOUR PRICE: $[Lower]
```

Rules: High perceived value + low delivery cost | Complementary to core | Quick wins | Logical progression

### Urgency & Scarcity Types

- **Time-based:** Countdown timers, specific deadlines, flash sales
- **Quantity-based:** Stock counters, "X of Y claimed," real-time notifications
- **Exclusive access:** Invite-only, VIP, first X customers
- **Seasonal/Event:** Black Friday, holiday, end-of-year

### Risk Reversal Hierarchy

Standard (30-day return) → Strong (60-day money-back) → Very confident (90-day) → Exceptional (1-year) → Ultimate ("Love it or don't pay")

### Offer Psychology

- **Anchoring:** Show original price, then discounted — $299 anchor makes $179 feel exceptional
- **Loss framing:** "Don't miss 40% off — ends tonight" > "Get 40% off today"
- **Reciprocity:** "As a thank you for subscribing, here's 20% off"
- **Social proof:** "427 people claimed this deal in the last 24 hours"

## Landing Page Congruence

### The Scent Trail

Consistent thread from ad → landing page → checkout: same colors, same hero image, same headline, same offer, same tone.

**Strong scent example:**

```
AD: "Get 50% Off Premium Yoga Mats - Today Only" + purple mat image
PAGE: "50% Off Premium Yoga Mats - Today Only" + same image + price + countdown + "Add to Cart - 50% Off"
```

**Broken scent:** Ad promises specific offer → Page shows homepage with no mention of offer → High bounce rate

### Landing Page Types

**Product Page:** Single product, clear buying intent. Above fold: hero image, price, CTA, 3-5 benefit bullets, rating.

**Lead Capture:** B2B, high-consideration. Minimal fields (3-5 max), form above fold, one clear CTA, no competing actions.

**Long-Form Sales:** Complex/high-ticket ($500+). Structure: Hook → Problem → Solution → Benefits → How It Works → Social Proof → About → Offer → Guarantee → FAQ → Urgency → Final CTA. Multiple CTAs every 1-2 scrolls.

**Webinar Registration:** Compelling headline (what they'll learn), date/time, registration form, speaker credentials, 3-5 learning bullets.

### Mobile Optimization

- Page load under 3 seconds
- Single column, 16px+ text, 48px+ buttons
- Minimal form fields (3 max), auto-fill enabled
- Most important info first, CTA above fold

### Common Congruence Mistakes

1. Generic landing page (ad→homepage instead of dedicated page)
2. Different offer on page vs. ad
3. Visual mismatch (different images)
4. Tone shift (casual ad → corporate page)
5. Hidden pricing
6. Increased friction (10-field form when ad said "instant access")
7. Bait and switch (conditions not mentioned in ad)

## Retargeting Creative Strategy

### Retargeting Funnel

| Level | Who | Time Since | Strategy | Message |
|-------|-----|-----------|----------|---------|
| 1. Site Visitors | Visited, didn't convert | 1-30 days | Remind, address objections, social proof | "Still thinking about it? Here's what you should know..." |
| 2. Engaged | Multiple pages, 2+ min | 1-14 days | Differentiation, stronger offers, case studies | "Here's why 10,000+ customers chose us over [Competitor]" |
| 3. Cart Abandoners | Added to cart | 1-7 days | Address final objections, strongest offers, urgency | "Complete your order in 24 hours and save 10%" |
| 4. Past Customers | Already purchased | Varies | Reorder, upsell, cross-sell, loyalty | "Customers who bought [A] love [B]. Get 30% off" |

### Best Practices

1. **Acknowledge the relationship** — Don't pretend it's first interaction
2. **Dynamic creative** — Show products they actually viewed (DPA/dynamic remarketing)
3. **Progression messaging** — Day 1-3: what makes it special → Day 4-7: what customers say → Day 8-14: save 15% → Day 15-30: 20% off welcome back
4. **Offer escalation** — Start soft, increase incentive over time
5. **Frequency capping** — Site visitors: 3-4/day | Engaged: 4-5/day | Cart abandoners: 5-6/day (first 48h)
6. **Exclude converters immediately**

### Retargeting Formulas

1. **Reminder + Social Proof:** "You checked out [Product]. Since then, 127 people bought it. Here's why..."
2. **Objection Handling:** "Still on the fence? Here's what other hesitant customers said: [testimonials addressing specific objections]"
3. **Urgency/Scarcity:** "Your cart is about to expire! Only 4 left in stock"
4. **Value Stack:** "Here's everything you get: [product + bonuses + guarantee + shipping]. Total value: $X. Your price: $Y"
5. **Comparison:** "Shopping around? Here's why 89% who compare us to competitors choose us"

### Performance Benchmarks (vs. Cold Traffic)

- CTR: 2-4x higher
- CPA: 30-50% lower
- Conversion Rate: 5-10x higher
- Cart abandoners: 10-30% CVR, 3-5x ROAS

## Ad Fatigue Prevention

### Detection

**Symptoms:** CTR declining >20% week-over-week | CPA increasing >25% | Frequency >5 | Relevance score dropping | Negative comments increasing

**Timeline:** Cold audiences fatigue in 7-14 days | Warm in 14-30 days | Small audiences (<50K) faster

### Prevention Strategies

**1. Creative Rotation:** Minimum 5 creatives per ad set. Weekly: add 2-3 new, remove bottom 2. Monthly: full audit, retire 30+ day ads.

**2. Hook Swapping (Video):** Keep video body, change first 3 seconds. Film 5-10 different hooks for each winning video. Looks fresh, 90% footage reused.

**3. Audience Expansion:** Larger audience = lower frequency. Expand lookalikes (1%→2-3%→5-10%), add related interests, test new geos.

**4. Frequency Management:** Cold: 4-5 impressions/week | Warm: 6-8/week | Hot: 8-10/week. Action when exceeded: expand audience, add creatives, reduce budget.

**5. Creative Lifespan Planning:** Day 1-14=launch | Day 15-30=peak | Day 31-45=optimal ROI | Day 46+=begin decline. At day 30 launch replacement, at day 45 shift budget, at day 60 retire.

**6. Modular Video:** Film 10 hooks + 3 body segments + 5 CTAs = 30+ combinations from one shoot.

### Response Matrix

| Fatigue Level | Symptoms | Action |
|--------------|----------|--------|
| Light | CTR down 10-20% | Add 2 new creatives |
| Moderate | CTR down 20-30%, CPA up 15-25% | Add 3-5 creatives + audience expansion |
| Severe | CTR down 30%+, CPA up 25%+, Frequency 7+ | Kill immediately, launch 5+ replacements |

### Production Volume

| Ad Spend | Monthly Creative Need |
|----------|---------------------|
| $10K/month | 20-30 creatives |
| $50K/month | 50-100 creatives |
| $200K+/month | 100-200+ creatives |

## Thumbnail Design Principles

### What Makes a Clickable Thumbnail

1. Visual intrigue | 2. Emotional trigger (face with expression) | 3. Curiosity gap | 4. Contrast (stands out) | 5. Clarity at small size

### Design Elements

**Faces:** Close-up (40-60% of thumbnail), exaggerated expressions, direct eye contact, high contrast from background.

**Text:** 3-6 words max, bold thick fonts (Impact, Montserrat Bold), high contrast (white+black outline), never cover face. Formulas: Result/Number ("$10K in 30 Days") | How-To | vs. Comparison | Mistake/Warning | Question

**Visual elements:** Arrows (draw attention), circles (emphasize detail), before/after splits, product showcase.

**Color:** Red=urgency | Yellow=attention | Blue=trust | Green=growth | Purple=creativity. High contrast pairs: Black+White, Yellow+Black, Red+White, Blue+Orange.

### Platform Specs

| Platform | Size | Key Rule |
|----------|------|----------|
| YouTube | 1280x720 (16:9), <2MB | 3 elements max (face, text, object) |
| FB/IG Feed | 1080x1080 (1:1) | First frame = thumbnail, sound-off design |
| Stories/Reels | 1080x1920 (9:16) | Keep elements away from top/bottom 250px |
| TikTok/Shorts | 9:16 | First frame IS the thumbnail (can't upload separate) |

### Templates

1. **Face + Text:** Face on one side (50%), text opposite, solid/blurred background
2. **Split Screen:** Before/After or A vs. B with clear center division
3. **Text-Dominant:** Bold text 60-70% of space, small supporting element
4. **Over-the-Shoulder:** Person looking at screen/object, viewer sees what they see
5. **Reaction:** Large expressive face + result/statement causing reaction

## Creative Performance Metrics

### Primary Metrics

| Metric | Formula | What Good Looks Like |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **CPA** | Spend ÷ Conversions | Below target, decreasing over time |
| **ROAS** | Revenue ÷ Ad Spend | 3-5x for e-commerce, above breakeven |
| **CTR** | (Clicks ÷ Impressions) × 100 | FB Feed 1.5-3%, Google Search 3-8%, TikTok 1-3% |
| **CVR** | (Conversions ÷ Clicks) × 100 | E-commerce 2-5%, Lead gen 5-15%, SaaS 3-10% |

### Video Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|--------|--------|
| Hook Rate (3s view) | >50% good, >60% great |
| Hold Rate (avg watch time) | 30-40% for feed, 50-70% for Stories/Reels |
| ThruPlay (completion or 15s) | Cost per ThruPlay <$0.05-0.10 |

### Cost Metrics

| Metric | Typical Range |
|--------|--------------|
| CPM (Facebook) | $5-15 |
| CPM (LinkedIn) | $30-100 |
| CPM (Google Display) | $2-10 |
| CPC | Varies; CPA = CPC ÷ CVR |

### Quality Rankings (Facebook)

- **Quality Ranking:** Ad quality vs. competitors for same audience
- **Engagement Rate Ranking:** Expected engagement vs. competitors
- **Conversion Rate Ranking:** Expected CVR vs. competitors

Higher relevance = lower costs + better delivery. Improve via: better creative, better targeting, better offer.

### Attribution Windows

```
Facebook: 7-day click, 1-day view (default)
Google: Data-driven (recommended), last click (default)
Different windows = different CPA reporting
```

### Monitoring Cadence

**Daily (15 min):** Spend, CPA/ROAS, volume, CTR drops
**Weekly (2 hours):** Creative winners/losers, frequency/fatigue, audience changes, quality rankings
**Monthly (half day):** Full account audit, winning/losing patterns, competitive benchmarks, YoY/MoM trends

### ROI Calculation

```
ROI = (Revenue - Ad Spend) ÷ Ad Spend × 100
Break-even ROAS = 1 ÷ Profit Margin (e.g., 40% margin → need 2.5:1 ROAS)
```

### Metrics by Objective

| Objective | Primary Metrics | Secondary |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| Awareness | CPM, Reach, Video Views | Engagement, Share rate |
| Consideration | CTR, CPC, Video Views | Engagement, Landing page views |
| Conversion | CPA, ROAS, CVR | CTR, CPC |
